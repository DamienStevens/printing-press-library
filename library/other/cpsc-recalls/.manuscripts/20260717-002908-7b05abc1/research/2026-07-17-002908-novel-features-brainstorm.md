## Customer model

### Household safety checker

**Today (without this CLI):** A parent or caregiver searches the CPSC website, email archives, and product labels whenever they review household or children's products. Product names vary between packaging and recall records, so they cannot tell whether a loose text match is meaningful without opening each recall and comparing model details manually.

**Weekly ritual:** Check recently purchased, inherited, or frequently used products against new recalls and preserve the remedy instructions for plausible matches.

**Frustration:** Search results blur exact identifier matches and fuzzy name similarities, making it hard to act confidently without repeating the evidence check by hand.

### Marketplace trust-and-safety analyst

**Today (without this CLI):** A retailer or marketplace analyst exports catalog rows, runs brand and keyword searches against recall feeds, and copies candidate matches into a review queue. They repeatedly normalize manufacturers, model names, and product descriptions, then recheck the same candidates when records change.

**Weekly ritual:** Screen the current catalog for recall candidates, investigate high-evidence matches, and document why each listing was flagged.

**Frustration:** Unstandardized product text creates false positives, while generic feeds do not retain field-level matching evidence or catalog-to-recall provenance.

### Institutional inventory manager

**Today (without this CLI):** A school, daycare, or property manager keeps an equipment spreadsheet and searches CPSC records product by product. When a recall is found, they assemble images, remedies, contact details, and affected inventory locations into a separate action note.

**Weekly ritual:** Recheck the managed inventory, identify newly relevant recalls, and distribute actionable packets for items requiring inspection or remedy.

**Frustration:** The inventory, recall evidence, and remedy instructions live in separate artifacts, so repeat checks and handoffs are slow and difficult to audit.

### Product-safety journalist

**Today (without this CLI):** A journalist or researcher downloads recall records and maintains notebooks that flatten nested hazards, remedies, incidents, manufacturers, and products. They compare recent windows manually and must avoid presenting recall counts as exposure-adjusted incident rates.

**Weekly ritual:** Review new and updated recalls, inspect hazard and remedy patterns, and preserve source-backed records for reporting.

**Frustration:** Nested records and changing recall content make trend summaries and update detection cumbersome, while the available data cannot support population-rate claims.
